# Pregnancy danger signs

Pregnant patients should be referred urgently if they have vaginal bleeding, severe abdominal pain, severe headache, blurred vision, convulsions, fainting, chest pain, shortness of breath, reduced fetal movement, or signs of severe infection.

Initial assessment should capture gestational age, bleeding amount, pain severity, blood pressure, temperature, and whether the patient can travel safely.

If there is heavy bleeding, severe pain, or loss of consciousness, do not delay referral for prolonged questioning.

# Pediatric fever quick guide

Children younger than 3 months with a temperature at or above 38.0 C should be referred for urgent medical assessment.

Red flags for fever include lethargy, seizures, trouble breathing, persistent vomiting, severe dehydration, inability to drink, stiff neck, non-blanching rash, or reduced responsiveness.

For stable children older than 3 months, collect:
- Temperature and duration of fever
- Fluid intake and urine output
- Breathing symptoms
- Vaccination status
- Exposure to sick contacts

Supportive advice may include oral fluids, cooling measures, and age-appropriate antipyretics if local guidance allows, but caregivers should be told to seek urgent help if red flags appear.

# Respiratory red flags

Shortness of breath, blue lips, inability to speak full sentences, oxygen saturation below 92 percent, chest pain, confusion, or rapidly worsening symptoms should trigger urgent escalation.

At intake, record respiratory rate, oxygen saturation if available, fever, cough duration, and exposure history.

If oxygen saturation is not available but the patient is visibly struggling to breathe, treat this as a high-risk presentation.

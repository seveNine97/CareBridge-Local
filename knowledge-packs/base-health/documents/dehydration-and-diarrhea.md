# Dehydration and diarrhea

Danger signs for dehydration include sunken eyes, lethargy, inability to drink, persistent vomiting, very little urine, weak pulse, cold extremities, or confusion.

Ask about stool frequency, vomiting, oral intake, urine output, fever, and whether the patient belongs to a higher-risk group such as infants, older adults, or pregnant patients.

Immediate referral is appropriate when dehydration is severe or the patient cannot keep fluids down.
